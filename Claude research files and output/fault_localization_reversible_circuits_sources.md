# Credible Sources for Fault Localization in Reversible Circuits

## Core Papers on Fault Localization

### 1. Fault Localization in Reversible Circuits is Easier than for Classical Circuits
**Authors:** Ramasamy, K., Tagare, R., Perkins, E., & Perkowski, M.  
**Publication:** Proceedings of the International Workshop on Logic and Synthesis (IWLS), June 2004  
**URL:** https://pdxscholar.library.pdx.edu/ece_fac/196/  
**Key Focus:** Demonstrates that fault localization is fundamentally simpler in reversible circuits compared to classical circuits

---

### 2. Fault Localization Scheme for Missing Gate Faults in Reversible Circuits
**Authors:** Various  
**Publication:** ACM Transactions on Design Automation of Electronic Systems  
**URL:** https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3503539  
**Key Focus:** Introduces a fault localization method to extract exact locations of single and multiple missing gate faults in k-CNOT-based reversible circuits

---

### 3. An Approach for Detection and Localization of Missing Gate Faults in Reversible Circuit
**Authors:** Mondal, B., Bandyopadhyay, C., Bhattacharjee, A., Roy, D., Parekh, S., & Rahaman, H.  
**Publication:** IETE Journal of Research, Vol 68, No 5, 2020  
**URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/03772063.2020.1772127  
**Key Focus:** Proposes fault detection and localization scheme for missing gate fault models with 100% accuracy

---

### 4. A Modified Technique for the Detection and Exact Localization of Missing Gate Faults in Reversible Circuits
**Authors:** Mondal, B., Datta, S., & Sarkar, S.  
**Publication:** IETE Journal of Research, 2025  
**URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03772063.2025.2511272  
**Key Focus:** Modified algorithm with 40% reduction in execution time for fault detection and localization

---

### 5. Detection and Localization of Appearance Faults in Reversible Circuits
**Publication:** IEEE Conference Publication, 7th International Symposium on Embedded Computing and System Design (ISED'17), 2017  
**URL:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/8303946  
**Key Focus:** Testing scheme for gate appearance and control appearance faults with single/n test vectors

---

## Stuck-at Faults and ATPG

### 9. Fault Testing for Reversible Circuits
**Authors:** Patel, K.N., Hayes, J.P., & Markov, I.L.  
**Publication:** arXiv preprint quant-ph/0404003, April 2004  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0404003  
**Key Focus:** Shows any test set detecting all single stuck-at faults also detects all multiple stuck-at faults

## Notes

- Most sources focus on k-CNOT (Toffoli) gate-based reversible circuits
- Many papers use benchmark circuits from RevLib for validation
- Recent research (2020-2025) shows improved efficiency with modified algorithms
- Strong connection between reversible circuit testing and quantum computing applications
